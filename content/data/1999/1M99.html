STEMBER, MATHIAS CAPTURE FLEET FEET CAPITOL MILE

     Local hero Michael Stember and Kristina Mathias of  Sunnyvale captured 
the open titles in the Fourth Annual Fleet Feet Capitol Mile,
a 1 mile road race, August 15th in downtown Sacramento, Calif. The race 
was the road mile championship for the Pacific Association of USA Track & 
Field and featured a $2,200 prize money purse, attracting more than 300 of 
the top runners in Northern California and Northern Nevada.
    The race featured 15 races of males and females, race walking, and 
running. A beautiful summer morning provided perfect running conditions for 
the flat, fast, and certified course near the State Capitol.
    Stember, a prep superstar at nearby Jesuit High School, pulled away from 
the men's field in the last half mile to win in 4:09.5. Earlier this month, 
Stember, an All America at Stanford, won the 1500 meter silver medal in the 
Pan-Am Games. Art Martin of Chico, a distant second in 4:18.7, won the $150 
first place prize money as Stember, a junior at Stanford University, is 
ineligible for the award.
    Kristina Mathias similarly dominated the women's open race, winning by 
more than 10 seconds in 5:02.4. Sheri Wright of Superior was second in 
5:13.5. Mathias earned $150 for the win.
    Top runners ages 40 and over were Steve Haas, 41, or Rocklin, 4:29.4, and 
Linda Gill of San Anselmo, 5:16.0.
    Age group course records were set by Margarethe Styskel of Murphys, 70, 
who ran  7:38.9; and men's race
walker Rick Holt of Visalia, 7:01.4.
    The event was sponsored by Fleet Feet Sports of Sacramento and New 
Balance Athletic Shoe Co.
