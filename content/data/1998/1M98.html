SORENSON, SPIES CAPTURE FLEET FEET CAPITOL MILE 
	SACRAMENTO, Calif.--Beckie Spies of San Francisco and Jim Sorenson of Castro Valley 
captured the open titles in the Third Annual Fleet Feet Capitol Mile, a 1 mile road race, 
July 19th in downtown Sacramento, Calif. The race was the road mile championship for the 
Pacific Association of USA Track & Field and featured a $2,000 prize money purse. More than
 425 runners and race walkers participated including many of top competitors in Northern 
California and Northern Nevada.
	The race featured 16 races of males and females, race walking, and running. The 
oppressive heat kept most course records safe for another year, but tight competition was
 the order of the day on the flat, fast certified course near the State Capitol. 
	Becky Spies pulled away from Lisa Daane of Reno, Nevada, to capture the women's
 title in 4:45.61. Daane was timed in 4:47.04. Spies earned $150 for the win.
	Sorenson out kicked Joe Carnegie of Fresno for the win in the men's open race,
 4:12.94 to 4:14.74. It was the third consecutive year Carnegie finished second. Sorenson
 also won $150 with Carnegie collecting $100.
	Dee Dee Grafius of Modesto successfully defended her women's masters title in 
5:15.12, collecting $100. Tim Minor of Reno won the men's 40-over title in 4:28.34.
	Top race walkers were Therese Iknoian of San Jose in 7:56.04 and Roger Wellborn of 
Sacramento in 7:49.16.
	The event was sponsored by Fleet Feet Sports of Sacramento and New Balance Athletic
 Shoe Co.
