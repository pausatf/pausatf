First 2008 Sanctioned Sectional PA/USATF Youth T&F Meet

Rain or Shine

Saturday, 1 March 2008
Oakmont High School
1710 Cirby Way, Roseville 95661

What do you need to know?

  • Cost: $6 per athlete (money order only, no club or personal checks accepted) no refunds
    • Mail To: RETC, 1442 Deerfield Circle, Roseville, CA 95747
  • Awards: Medals to Top 3 overall while Top 6 in each heat / flight will receive a ribbon (given after each heat is completed)
  • Results: Results will be posted within 30-minutes after each event is over and on the PA/USATF website within 2-days after the meet is completed
  • Snack Bar: RETC will provide a snack bar with hot meal-deals for breakfast & lunch (school does not allow BBQs to be brought on campus)
  • USATF Membership: PA/USATF Board voted that beginning with the 2007 T&F season all youth athletes must join PA/USATF before they can compete in a sanctioned USATF meet. Join before coming to the meet or at the meet.
    • NOTE: If a club is not a registered 2008 club in good standing then all athletes associated with that club will need to transition to another club or compete unattached. If the athlete chooses to compete unattached then they should not wear their club uniform (simple as turning it inside out if necessary)
  • Registration: Online registration must be received by midnight February 23. Same day registration = $12 per athlete and not accepted after 7:00 am morning of the meet. Check the RETC website (www.eteamz.com/RosevilleExpress) to confirm unattached and club entries have been received. Use Club Manager to submit entries via Craig (meet timer) at redcaptiming@gmail.com
    • If you have never used Club Manager before contact Charlie Sheppard at charlie.sheppard.home@worldnet.att.net or 408-629.2617 to obtain a copy of the software and instructions on how to load and use it on your computer
  • Relays: An unattached athlete may not compete on a relay team (this is a USATF rule); clubs attending the meet are requested to provide 1-2 officials at the time of the relays to assist with relay exchange zone judging (meet at the finish line prior to the start of the first relay); remind your athletes to pull their tape from the track after they complete a relay exchange
  • Seeding: Our best efforts will be made to seed heats so that two athletes from the same club will not be in the same heat. Since this is the first sectional meet of the season seeding times / distances are not necessary when you register
    • Last heat of each age group in 100m, 200m and 400m is fast heat. If you have a youth athlete that should be in the fast heat ensure you instruct your athlete to notify the clerk area when they check in
  • Volunteers: RETC will be providing all volunteers to run this meet. We want coaches and parents to be able to focus on coaching & cheering everyone as they compete; however, if you see an area that is struggling bring it to the attention of the meet director or jump in and help
  • Access to infield: No coaches on infield except coaches clearly displaying 2008 PA/USATF membership ID cards and assisting Sub-Bantam and Bantam long jumpers setting marks. Coaches must leave when the event begins
  • Questions: Contact Maura Kent @ bookoutgen@yahoo.com or (916) 786-7288
  • Dogs: School does not allow dogs on campus


Driving Directions

  • From Reno: Take I-80 West towards Sacramento. Exit Riverside Blvd., turn right at off ramp onto Riverside Blvd. (staying in right lane). At next stop light turn right onto Cirby Way. Follow till you reach Oakmont HS on the right-side of the street (~1.5 miles)
  • From San Francisco: Take I-80 East. When you enter the outskirts of Sacramento stay to the right and take the I-80 Reno split. (If you stay on Business I-80 through Sacramento you’ll add 20-30 minutes to your commute.) Exit on Auburn Blvd. /. Riverside (Exit 102). Stay in the right lane of the two exit lanes from the freeway heading towards Auburn Blvd. basically you’ll go through the off ramp stop light heading straight (crossing Auburn Blvd.) and now you’ll be on Orlando Ave. (frontage road paralleling the freeway). Orlando Ave. will dead-end at Cirby Way (stop light). Turn right on Cirby Way and travel for 1.1 miles. OHS will be on your right-hand side.
  • From Modesto: Take I-99 North. I-99 will merge with Business I-80 as you enter downtown Sacramento. At the merge stay to the far left merging onto I-80 to Reno (basically you’ll stay straight on the freeway). Stay on I-80 till you reach Auburn Blvd. / Riverside Ave. exit. Follow directions above from San Francisco.

Order of Events

Depending on number of athletes in each event the Race Walks and 3000m / 5000m runs may have all age groups compete in one heat, during designated time in schedule. Additional races could be combined depending on number of athletes.

Order of running and field events are Girls then Boys

This year RETC is trying something new. For the Bantam, Midget & Youth age groups we will offer a Multi competition along with regular events. Athletes who sign up for the Multi will not do any other event at the meet. Athletes in the Multis will stay as a group and progress from one event to the next. They will transition into field / running events between existing events on the schedule. Order of events:

Multis
  • Bantam: High Jump, Shot Put, 200m (girls) / 400m (boys)
  • Midget: 80m Hurdles, Shot Put, High Jump, Long Jump, 800m (girls) / 1500m (boys)
  • Youth: 100m Hurdles, Shot Put, High Jump, Long Jump, 800m (girls) / 1500m (boys)
