PACIFIC ASSOCIATION J.O. XC CHAMPIONSHIP
November 11, 2001
New Records, New Champions!!!

The 2001 USATF/Pacific Association Jr. Olympic Cross Country Championship at Sierra College in Rocklin, CA on November 11, 2001 presented the best XC
competition we've seen in our association so far this season.  With nearly
250 entrants, the races were definitely competitive and exciting to watch. 
We saw three old meet records fall:

BANTAM GIRLS 3K
Old Record - 13:10.90 - KYLIE BROWN/Buffalo Chips
New Record - 12:24.09 - HELEN SELDIN/Buffalo Chips 
             12:34.45 - JESSICA RUX/Unattached
             12:45.58 - DANZEL BRADSHAW/Vallejo PAL Steppers
             12:58.31 - HALEY BRISCOE/Buffalo Chips

YOUTH GIRLS 4K
Old Record - 15:41.60 - ERIN WACHTER/Buffalo Chips
New Record - 15:07.45 - KARA ANDERSON/Acorn
             15:11.85 - CHANTEL WILLIAMS/Silver State

YOUTH BOYS 4K
Old Record - 13:39.69 - JOSEPH PARKER/Silver State
New Record - 13:39.40 - TIM CANNON/Buffalo Chips

Also, this season welcomed a new effort to promote more participation in
youth XC as points were accumulated based on finish positions at all the
preliminary meets this season.  Award winners from each division were
presented with a high quality, sculpture-type trophy of a runner (selected
by Bob Shor).  Congratulations to these winners:

Bantam Girls      Helen Seldin, Buffalo Chips
Bantam Boys       Ryan Lok, Unattached
Midget Girls      Renisha Robinson, UMOJA
Midget Boys       Nate Beach, Diablo Valley  
Youth Girls       Caprice Bradshaw, Vallejo PAL Steppers
Youth Boys        Tim Cannon, Buffalo Chips
 
Once again, as in the years previous, MANY THANKS to the VOLUNTEERS whose
willingness and expertise made it possible to keep the meet running on
schedule; the PARENTS and COACHES who dedicate so much of their time to this
great bunch of kids, and to our INCREDIBLE ATHLETES who are really
responsible for giving us an opportunity to make things like this happen.  

(Special thanks to a couple of our most INSPIRATIONAL Volunteers -- Buffalo
Chip Senior Runners Marv Johnson and Ron Peck.  Marv turns 70 in a few weeks,
and this past September he completed his tenth consecutive 52 mile trek of
the Sierra Nevada Endurance Run.  Ron, who turns 60 in February, ran the
Helen Klein 50 miler the day before coming out to be the chase bike for our
eight separate races on this hilly course.)  

INSPIRED AGAIN, 

Ken Briscoe, Buffalo Chips, Sacramento, CA
