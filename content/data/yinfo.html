5/27/06
Anthony's Quick Club Manager Meet Entry
 
Read through the documentation because there is initial setup you must do 
before entering athlete(s) [takes less than one minute].  Once the setup 
is done, enter the athlete(s) information and event(s) into the program 
by clicking on the "add new member" button.  When done with that, go back 
to the main screen and click on the "enter meet" button which is on the 
same line as the athlete(s) name.  This must be done for each athlete 
entering the meet.  On the bottom of the screen you can then enter the 
athlete(s) into the events.  Check the meet entries by clicking on the 
"show meet entries" button on the main screen and verifying that all 
entries are correct.  If something is wrong  go back to "enter meet" 
button and make the necressary changes.  Come back to verify that 
everything is correct.  If it is, go back to the main screen, click 
on "create meet manager file", name it appropriately, save it to a 
location you can easily find and email it to 
Anthony Camargo at ajcam@sbcglobal.net.  
 
Anthony Camargo

you recently received by e-mail. The program will fail if entering more 
than one relay team into a meet. The file in the link below will correct 
the problem.
 
Download the file in the link below into the folder that you extracted the 
clubmanager program into.  Before you download it, check to see if you have
already created meet entries that you want to save: startup clubmanager then 
click on the button labeled Show meet entries. This will display all the 
meet entries that you have created so far.
 
If there are no records showing, then no further action is required. 
If records are showing that you wish to save, then click on the button 
labeled Save meet to file. Once you have saved the entries then close 
down clubmanager and then download the file from link below.

then start clubmanager again and goto the show meet entries. On this screen
click on the button labeled Restore a meet from file. Restore the file 
just saved. This procedure will restore clubmanager to its status prior

March 15, 2006

Dear Youth Committee:

I would like to thank all the clubs, parents and coaches who came out to 
help at the first track and field meet of our 2006 track and field season.  
Without your help it would not have been possible to have any semblance of 
a track meet.  Unfortunately things did not go as well as expected.

My apologies for any and everything that went wrong.  It was not my 
intention to host a bad track meet.  This was my first time as a youth 
Meet Director and I truly wanted to do things right.  Unfortunately I 
did not appreciate the degree and complexity of even a "small" youth meet.  
My lack of preparedness severely compromised the quality of the meet.  
This has been a difficult learning experience and I vow to never allow 
anything of this nature to happen again. 

The youth teams of the PAUSATF normally give good meets.  SO, for those 
first time parents and athletes, please do not let this experience dampen
your enthusiasm.   

For those athletes who did not pick up their ribbons, you may pick them up
at the East Bay Heat meet on March 25th at Berkeley High School.  I will 
be helping out at the awards station.  If you have additional questions 
or concerns, please feel free to e-mail me at sampledk@yahoo.com and I 
will be more than happy to respond.

Again, thank you all for coming and hope to see you at other PAUSATF 
youth meets.

Sincerely,

Deborah Sample
Meet Director, Pinole Valley Classic Sectional

Hasay named Athlete of the Week
12/14/04
 
INDIANAPOLIS - Jordan Hasay of Arroyo Grande, Calif., has been named USA
Track & Field's Athlete of the Week after winning the Youth Girl's race
Saturday at the 2004 USATF National Junior Olympic Cross Country
Championships in Shaumburg, Illinois.
 
More than 2,500 athletes age 8 to 18 and 200 teams from all 57 USATF
Associations competed for boys' and girls' titles Saturday in 10 age
divisions in cold, windy and muddy conditions.
 
Competing in the youth girls division, the 13-year-old Hasay entered the
championships as the nation's top runner in her age group. At the 2004
USATF National Junior Olympic Track & Field Championships this summer,
she set national records in the 1,500 and 3,000 meters, and in August
she set a pending 13-year-old 5 km road best of 17:05. Saturday in
Shaumburg, she opened up a 20-second lead in the first half-mile of the
race before cruising to a 24-second victory with a time of 14:19 over 4
km. For her outstanding performance, Hasay was named the Verizon Girl's
Athlete of the Meet.
 
Now in its fourth year, USATF's Athlete of the Week program is designed
to recognize outstanding performers at all levels of the sport. USATF
names a new honoree each week and features the athlete on the USATF
website. Selections are based on top performances and results from the
previous week.

PA/USATF Teams and Athletes do well at Nationals
12/13/04

 Jordan Hasay of Arroyo Grande, Calif., and Kevin Havel of Arlington
 Heights, Ill., were named Verizon Athletes of the meet at Saturday's
 USATF National Junior Olympic Cross Country Championships, held at Busse
 Woods Forest Preserve in Shaumburg, Illinois.
 
 More than 2,500 athletes age 8 to 18 and 200 teams from all 57 USATF
 Associations competed for boys' and girls' titles in 10 age divisions in
 cold, windy and muddy conditions. Team champions also were crowned in
 those divisions, as well as in a new high school age division. Age
 divisions were bantam, born in 1994-95 and racing over 3 kilometers;
 midget, born in 1992-93 and racing 3 km; youth, born in 1990-92 and
 racing 4 km; intermediate, born in 1988-89 and racing 5 km; young men
 and women, born in 1986-87 and racing 5 km, and high school, racing 5
 km.
 
 Competing in the youth girls division, the 13-year-old Hasay entered the
 championships as the nation's top runner in her age group. At the 2004
 USATF National Junior Olympic Track & Field Championships this summer,
 she set national records in the 1,500 and 3,000 meters, and in August
 she set a pending 13-year-old 5 km road best of 17:05. Saturday in
 Shaumburg, she opened up a 20-second lead in the first half-mile of the
 race before cruising to a 24-second victory with a time of 14:19 over 4
 km.
 
 Also a youth division athlete, 14-year-old Kevin Havel of Prospect
 Heights RC earned boys' athlete of the meet honors with his victory,
 which came not long after he administered the athletes' pledge during
 Opening Ceremonies for the Championships. The high school freshman won
 his 4 km divisional race Saturday in 12:50. Havel's victory came on the
 heels of an impressive, 5th-place finish November 6 at the Illinois High
 School State 2A Championships.
 
 Other individual winners for girls included Kaylor Murray (Nashville,
 Ill.) in the bantam division, 10:58; Nicole Hood (Pleasanton Heat) in
 midget 10:46; Bria Wetsch (unattached - Minnesota) in intermediate,
 18:08; Claire Maduza (unattached - Wisconsin) in young women, 18:23; and
 Maria Vinton (Sanger High) in high school, 22:41. Girls' team champions
 were Oregon Cross Country in bantam, Transy East TC in midget, DC Red
 Wings TC in Youth, Buffalo Chips in intermediate and young women, and
 Sanger High in High School.
 
 Individual boys' winners were Darren Fahy (San Diego SoCal RoadRun) in
 bantam, 10:27; Jared Kenney (San Diego SoCal Road Run) in midget, 9:53;
 Bobby Moldovan (Team Bird) in intermediate, 15:43; Alexander Miletich
 (Unattached - Florida) in young men, 15:37; and Jean Ambler (Christian
 Heritage) in high school, 18:55. Team champions were San Diego SoCal
 RoadRun in bantam and midget, Oregon Cross Country in youth, Team Idaho
 in intermediate, Silver State Striders in young men, and Christian
 Heritage in high school.
