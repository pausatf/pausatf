Certification: Jim Hume.

Nothing seems to be happening with national in the way of certifications, especially in the area of electronic measuring, recording and judging. Jim Hume pointed out that he should have been certified in this field a long time ago and all the names submitted from the NCAA championships this summer have received no action from national as far as he can determine.

Jim Hume fielded a question from Dick Connors regarding two individuals who became certified this past year but are not listed as members on the spreadsheet kept by Jim Hume. It was pointed out that becoming certified in no way makes a person a member. Membership depends upon payment of membership fees each year and if those fees are not paid, they will not be reported as members for purposes of mailings and other benefits of membership of PAUSATF. George Kleeman pointed out that there are presently about 15 to 20 individuals who claim to be members but are not certified, ie., they never went through the process to become recertified at the beginning of the Olympiad. There seems to be three classifications of people in the organization at present: Those that have paid their membership and are certified; those that paid membership but are not certified and those that are certified but have not paid their membership. It was agreed that the two individuals in question would be further researched by the certification chair.

Old Business:

Training Clinics:

Basically, George Kleeman said, he and his committee need some direction from this board on what direction they should go in.

The subcommittee received about 20 responses from the approximately 300 surveys that were sent out.

INTRODUCTORY CLINIC: There will be a general introductory clinic taking up both the morning and afternoon sessions with the certification exam at the end. This clinic will not be as specific and detailed on specific events as introductory clinics in the past. After becoming certified, the member may then take a more specific second tiered advance basic clinic in either Track or Field events. These clinics will be only a morning or afternoon session and it is hoped the member will avail himself/herself of these clinics only after some exposure to track and field the first year after becoming certified.

For some modicum of uniformity and consistency of information conveyed in all clinics, it is planned to have a central place to store outlines of the individual clinics submitted by the instructors and available to all the instructors. The idea is that the instructors are to write down the goals of the clinic he/she leads and the outline and material used in the clinic. This is a 5 year plan to collect these outlines headed up by Dennis Boyle at the present. Also included in this phase of the project are Jim Hume, Shirley Connors, Dick Connors, Bruce Colman, Margaret Sheehan and George Kleeman.

Next year, after the Bejing Olympics, will be a re-certification year and plans must be made for how we will conduct the re-certification. It is proposed that the clinics will be done in groups as was done last time. Instead of multiple tests for individuals, a person being recertified will only take one test – at the highest level of certification he/she has obtained or applying for. New this time will be a Referee exam with 5 association level exams.

Rule Books: John Wise and Phil Leake have agreed to handle obtaining and distributing rule books this year. Members may obtain rule books by buying directly from the publisher (NCAA, etc.) or online through active.com which will add $2 and those books can be delivered to the member at a clinic. If the member wants it mailed to them there will be a $2 handling fee added to the cost. If there are any “extra” books they will be on sale at the clinics on a first come, first serve basis, usually sold out after the ARC clinics.

George Kleeman has already prepared both the Association level and National level test for USATF and they will be available by December 31, 2007. These tests can be used by the various associations if they choose. Jim Hume is requesting help from Master level officials in preparing our own tests. There will be a new officials test which will have the book and page number where the answer can be found and the association level test which will have the same type of questions but the member will have to find the answer in the appropriate book by themselves.

Dick Connors questioned the giving a clinic “Youth vs High School Rules”. George Kleeman responded that the clinics that are listed are the ones that were requested through the survey his committee sent to the membership. If there are any that should not be taught or should be changed, that can be accomplished. Dick Connors emphasized that a curriculum should be developed for each clinic geared to the learner and the instructors should not preach. Once the curriculum is established the instructor then must determine how he/she will teach the subject.

George Kleeman stated that no manuals will be given out at the introductory classes. However, all participants at the introductory classes will get all three rule books (High School, NCAA and USATF, a $29 value, which will be included in the $35 registration fee. This is an increase of $10 over last year but all three rule books will be included. Manuals will not be provided at the upper level clinics but will be online and the instructors can print them out and copy them for the clinic or the students can do the same.

George Kleeman pointed out if active.com is used for the purchase of books, there will be a service fee of $3 per transaction.

After discussion it was decided that the Introductory Clinic would cost $35 per registrant if pre registered and $45 if registering at the door. Referee, Hy-Tek and Advance Basic Clinics would cost $13 pre registered and $23 at the door. All other clinics would cost $8 pre registered and $18 at the door.

Jim Hume indicated that he had heard that there were problems using active.com but he did not have any specifics about what the problems may be.

John Murray moved to adopt active.com for registration for clinics and for purchasing rule books for the minimum period of the contract. Dick Connors seconded the motion.

Discussion: Jim Hume asked if there were any other services like active.com. Bruce Colman indicated that there were but this was the most used one for what we were doing.

Jon Siegel asked if the information regarding registration could be downloaded more than one time. George Kleeman indicated that the information remains on the server and those with access can download the same information as many times as he/she wants.

Mike Leonard raised the issue of what ID theft security features are in place to protect our members. Bruce Colman and George Kleeman assured that the information that would be on the server would be minimal and active.com has systems installed to protect against ID theft.

George Kleeman called the question. In Favor: All
