CONDON SETS MEN'S 65-69 AGE GROUP RECORD AT

USA MASTERS OUTDOOR TRACK & FIELD CHAMPIONSHIPS

Frank Condon

 

FOLSOM, CALIF. - August 5, 2007 - Pacific Association USA Track & Field (PA/USATF) athletes excelled at the USA Master Outdoor Track & Field Championships, held August 2-5 at the University of Maine in Orono, Maine.

Frank Condon of Chico, Calif. set a U.S. men's age group record for 65-69 year-old men for 800 meters. Condon's new standard is 2 minutes, 17.20 seconds. Condon also won the M65 1,500 meter race in 4:58.06. 

Louise Walters of Foresthill, Calif. captured the W65 5,000 meter race walk in 31:57.91 and the W65 10,000m race walk in 1:03:42.01. Greenbrae, Calif. athlete Jack Brae won both the M70 5,000m race walk in 29:51.25 and the M70 10,000m race walk in 1:02:56.54. John Levinsohn of Greenbrae, Calif. topped the M85 5,000m race walk in 45:54.75.

Edythe Leek of Sacramento, Calif. won the W75 outdoor pentathlon, and the W75 200m dash in 41.97. 

Tom Bernhard of Fremont, Calif. doubled with wins in the M55 5,000-meter race (17:33.56) and the M55 10,000-meter race (36:22.70).

Tom Bernhard

Liz Palmer of Folsom, Calif. was a winner in the W45 short hurdles with a mark of 13.29.

Lorraine Tucker of San Jose, Calif. won the W60 discus throw in 28.28 meters/92-09.25 feet. Tucker also placed second in the W60 shot put with a put of 9.03m. Thomas Fahey of Chico, Calif. turned in the winning M55 discus throw with a toss of 50.04m/164-02.0.

Michael Venning of Cupertino, Calif. took the Gold in the M45 Hammer Thriow (45.60m/149-07).

The Sacramento-based Golden West Athletics club won the W40-49 4x100 Meter Relay in 58.72 seconds and the M60-69 4x800 Meter Relay in 11 minutes, 22.59 seconds.

More than 1,100 of the nation's finest masters athletes (ages 30 to 95-plus) were in Orono competing in the national championships. Masters athletes now prepare for the World Masters Athletics Championships, September 4-15 in Riccione, Italy.
