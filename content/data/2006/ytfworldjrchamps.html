Friday, July 21, 2006

Team USA roster announced for the 2006 IAAF World Junior
Championships

INDIANAPOLIS - USA Track & Field announced the Team USA roster for the
11th IAAF World Junior Championships, held August 15-20, in Beijing,
China. The Team will also culminate their training camp by competing
alongside some of the nation's top elite athletes at the Road to Eugene
'08 on August 8 at Hayward Field in Eugene, Ore.

A total of 82 athletes - 43 men and 39 women - will be part of the U.S.
delegation for the World Junior Championships. The U.S. roster was
determined at the 2006 Finish Line USA Junior Track & Field
Championships, June 21-25, in Indianapolis, Indiana.

Morocco medalists lead the way

2005 World Youth 400-meter hurdle champion and USATF Youth Athlete of
the Year Ebony Collins will try to match her record-setting performance
from last year but will face stiff competition from teammate and 2006
USA Junior champion Nicole Leach.

Other athletes to watch include 2005 World Youth and 2006 Nike Outdoor
Nationals and USA Junior 110-meter hurdle champion Cordera Jenkins; 2005
World Youth pole vault silver medalist Scott Roth; 2005 World Youth 100m
champion and 200m silver medalist Bianca Knight; and 2005 World Youth
discus bronze medalist Kamorean Hayes.

Nation's junior elite headline roster

2006 NCAA and 2005 Pan Am Junior champion Rebekah Noble will face the
world's best junior women in the 800 meters. Noble is the first freshman
ever to win an NCAA title.

Also look for BYU freshman Chris Carter, who took 3rd in the 400-meter
hurdles as the NCAA Champs; 2006 Nike Outdoor Nationals 2,000m
steeplechase champion Marie Lawrence; 2006 Nike Outdoor Nationals 100m
champion Gabrielle Mayo; 2006 USA Junior Outdoor 100m and 200m champion
Willie Perry, who took fifth in the 200m at NCAA Champs; 2005 Foot
Locker XC and Nike Outdoor National 2-mile champion AJ Acosta in the
1,500m; and 2006 Nike Outdoor National champion Walter Henning, who set
an American junior record in the hammer throw at the 2006 Finish Line
USA Junior Championships.

Robert Weir, the Head Track and Field Coach at Stanford and the men's
throws coach for the U.S. team at the 2005 IAAF World Championships,
will lead the men's team in Beijing, while University of Texas at San
Antonio distance coach Rose Monday will head the women's team.
