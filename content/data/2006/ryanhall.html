Tuesday, February 21, 2006
Hall named Athlete of the Week

INDIANAPOLIS - Ryan Hall has been named USA Track and Field's Athlete of the Week after winning the open men's 12 km race on Sunday at the 2006 USA Cross Country Championships, at Van Cortland Park in New York.

The 2005 USA Outdoor bronze medalist at 5,000m, Hall finished the race in 34 minutes, 38 seconds -- 27 seconds ahead of runner-up Jorge Torres. The previous day Hall placed fourth in the open men's 4 km race in 10:57. With his first and third place finishes, Hall is eligible to compete at the World Cross Country Championships in Fukuoka, Japan in both the 4 km and the 12 km.

Elsewhere, American pole vault record holder Jeff Hartwig, 38, continued his amazing 2006 indoor season Saturday with a world best clearance of 5.85 meters/19 feet, 2.25 inches at the John Dalton Memorial Open in Vermillion, S.D. His previous season's best of 5.73m/18-9.50 came February 3 in winning the Millrose Games in New York.

Milton Campbell also set world leaders Saturday in Blacksburg, Va., running 200m in 20.42 (indoor personal best) and 400m in 45.81, on a standard 200m indoor oval. Not to be outdone, two-time Olympic silver medalist Terrence Trammell set the men's 60m hurdles world leader with his win at Bloomington, Ind., in 7.51 seconds, and Central Michigan University senior Johnnie Drake set the world's best 60m time this indoor season with his victory at Mt. Pleasant, Mich., in 6.53 seconds.

Bianca Knight, a junior at Ridgeland (Miss.) High School, won 60- and 200-meter sprints Saturday in the Simplot Games at Pocatello, Idaho. She set a personal best in the 60m with a 7.25 clocking and set a meet record in the 200m with a time of 23.26. More than 2,000 athletes from the United States and Canada participated in the Games.

Now in its sixth year, USATF's Athlete of the Week program is designed to recognize outstanding performers at all levels of the sport. USATF names a new honoree each week and features the athlete on the USATF website. Selections are based on top performances and results from the previous week.

2006 USATF Athlete of the Week Winners: January 3, Walt Henning; January 10, Amandi Rhett; January 17, Kevin Eastler; January 24, Marisa Hanson; January 31, Me'Lisa Barber; February 7, Reese Hoffa; February 14, Wallace Spearmon; February 21, Ryan Hall.
