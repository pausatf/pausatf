Dear PAUSATF Clubs, Coaches, Parents and Athletes:

 

Welcome!!  The Pacific Association of USA Track and Field welcomes you to the Pacific Association Youth Track and Field Championship Meet.  The competition will be held Saturday and Sunday June 10th and 11th at Pinole Valley High School in Pinole.  For all questions and the latest updates regarding the 2006 Pacific Association Youth Championship Meet, please contact Meet Directors:

Our very own Charlie Sheppard is the author of the Club Manager track team organizer program.  We are using this new computer program to submit all entries for the PAC, JO and Subbantam championship meets starting this year.  Please contact Joanne Slaton-Camargo if you did not participate at any of the previously scheduled training sessions for Club Manager earlier this year.  You will then receive a copy of the program via email.  Refer to the instructional word document included to help you get started using this simple program.  All entries to our above mentioned championship meets MUST be submitted via email using Club Manager.   When you receive your copy of the program, included you will also receive contact information for Charlie Sheppard.  If you have any questions regarding the use of Club Manager, he will be more than happy to help you.

 

Entry Alternative to Club Manager

As members of the Youth Committee we are again putting forth a group effort to host a series of five youth championship meets this year.  They consist of the PAC Championships at Pinole Valley High School in Pinole, the JO Championships at Granada High School in Livermore, the Subbantam Championships at Pinole Valley High School, the Region 14 Junior Olympic Championships at Granada High School in Livermore and the Region 14/Junior Olympic Multi Event Championships at James Logan High School in Union City. The volunteer lists for each meet will be available on the www.pausatf.org website.  Individuals and teams can then easily determine which meets they wish to help work. Together we will make this years’ meets a quality experience for all involved; athletes, parents, coaches, volunteers, family and friends.   Thank you so much in advance for your forthcoming participation and cooperation.  Let’s have five great championship track and field meets again in 2006!

Entries – The deadline for receipt of entries via email is Thursday, June 1, 2006.  Teams should set their club up using the Club Manager program right now.  After putting all information into your Club Manager program, email your completed track meet information directly to Anthony Camargo at ajcam@sbcglobal.net.  Entries submitted after the June 1st deadline will be assessed a $10.00 per event late fee = $16.00 per event.  Day of meet entries are $20.00 per event late fee = $26.00 per event.

 

Please check the PAUSATF website for your teams’ 4-digit code.   Click on the “Youth” section under “Club Manager”. Your 4-digit code is located there.  Example:  3M Track Club = 3MTC; Central Valley Roadrunners = CVRR; Roseville Express Track Club = RETC.  This code is needed to use the Club Manager program properly.  Unattached Athletes must also use the Club Manager program.  Your “team” abbreviation is UNA; you are the only 3-digit code users.  

 

Three events maximum for Subbantams, Bantams and Midgets; four events maximum for Youth, Intermediates and Young Men/Women.  A relay is an event.  Only registered PA/USATF clubs can enter a relay team.  Unattached athletes cannot run relays.

 

Entry fee per athlete is $6.00 per event and must be received no later than Monday, June 5, 2006.  Any entry fees received after that date will be considered late and athletes are susceptible to denial into the track meet. Do not mail in cash.  Personal Checks will not be accepted.  Entry fees must be paid by Postal Money Order or Certified Check made out to "Pacific Association / USATF."  Do not send in a manner that might require a signature for receipt.  This is not a business and there will be no one available to sign for your envelope.  Therefore, mail your entry fees by regular US mail only to:

 

·        Mike Hammerquist

USATF and birth verification:  If you need a USATF number for your athlete(s), contact the Pacific Association office immediately to get that taken care of in time to meet the entry deadline or for faster results register online at www.pausatf.org, click “Join Now” and follow the directions.

 

A copy of the birth certificate for each athlete is needed if not already verified via Tony Williams from last year’s 2005 season.  All birth certificates from last year are still on record and    verified.  You will not need to submit another birth certificate if you have previously done so for an athlete.  Please check with Tony Williams now at judgetonytracknfield@yahoo.com

           

Packet Pick-Up - Team coaches will be able to pick up their competition numbers and related materials at Pinole Valley High school.  Pick-up table will be open at 7:00 a.m.  Arrive early enough to get this process out of the way before the meet begins. 

 

Athlete's Check-In Time for Events

  • Athletes must check in anytime between the first and final call.  Coaches must have their athletes checked into these designated areas no later than five (5) minutes after the FINAL CALL for their event.   Athletes should then remain in the clerking area. After the event is closed and heat sheets are drawn, no changes will be made for any running events.  PERIOD!  Field event athletes must check in at their particular field event.
  • Results and Performance Lists - After the entries have been entered into the computer, PERFORMANCE LISTS will be posted.  Following the meet, check the Pacific Association website (www.pausatf.org) or look for them on the wall at Pinole Valley High the day of the meet.  During the meet, running event lane assignments will be posted as soon as possible after the athletes have checked in.  Results will be posted as soon after the competition as possible.  The posted results are for the benefit of everyone so please do not remove them.

 

Implements – Field event implements except javelins and pole vault poles, will be supplied by meet management if necessary.  Personal implements must be passed by our Weights & Measures official.  All implements must be checked between 7:30 - 11:00 am.

 

Spike Length – Maximum 3/16" pyramid spikes on all synthetic rubber surfaces.  Athletes that do not have the proper spikes will be asked to obtain the correct spikes or they will not be allowed to compete.

 

Bib Numbers & Hip Numbers – Bib numbers will be distributed at packet pick-up. All athletes must have a bib number assigned to them in order to compete. Any lost or forgotten bib numbers will result in a $5.00 replacement fee. Additionally, in the track events, all athletes will be assigned a hip number when they check in with the clerk for the final seeding.

 

Starting Heights - The starting height for the high jump and the pole vault will be per USATF rule 242.5(k).  Incremental increases will be 5cm in the high jump and 15cm in the pole vault. 

 

Protest Table - All protests must be submitted in writing within thirty (30) minutes after the results on an event are posted per rule 73.  This time period will be strictly enforced.  There will be a $50.00 cash submission fee required to file a protest.  This fee will be refunded only if the protest is upheld.  Only violations of the USATF 2006 Rules of Competition may be protested.  Judgment calls made by meet officials cannot be protested.  Protests that do not meet the above criteria will not be accepted.  The location of the protest table will be announced.

 

Awards - Awards will be available for pick by the coaches or athletes at the Awards table one hour after the results have been posted. Medals are awarded to the top six places in each event

           

Medical Services & Security - There will be a doctor or other qualified personnel available at all sessions of the championships.  Additional medical facilities are within minutes of the stadium and the local Fire Department and Rescue Unit is located minutes from the stadium.

 

Stadium Rules:

 

TENTS:  Allowed on the top row of the bleachers only but cannot block the announcer’s area.  Tents must be broken down and removed from the stadium at the completion of competition each day.  Pinole Valley High School and USATF is not responsible for lost or stolen items. 

 

ALCOHOL:  There is no alcohol permitted on school grounds.

 

SMOKING :  The school is a SMOKE FREE ZONE.  There is no smoking allowed inside or immediately outside the stadium.

 

COOLERS: Permitted inside the stadium; however, GLASS CONTAINERS in the coolers ARE NOT PERMITTED.  Coolers will be checked at the gate.  Please remove any alcohol, tobacco or glass before entering the stadium.

 

Admission – Spectators admitted free

 

Parking - There will be FREE PARKING available at and around the competition site. DO NOT PARK IN THE LIBRARY PARKING LOT ACROSS THE STREET FROM THE SCHOOL; YOU WILL BE TICKETED AND TOWED.  OVERFLOW PARKING IS LOCATED AT THE ALBERTSON STORE PARKING LOT DOWN THE STREET.

 

Food Services – Food and beverages will be sold throughout the meet.  You may bring your own food and drink into the stadium but you may not bring in glass containers.  When you leave, you must remove anything you brought in with you.  For your convenience, plastic garbage bags and garbage cans will be available throughout the facility for garbage disposal. 

 

T-shirt Concessions will be available at the meet

 

Lost and Found - A lost and found area will be located near the protest table

GUIDELINES FOR P.A. YOUTH CHAMPIONSHIPS

 

1. All athletes must be registered members of USA Track & Field in order to enter the meet. The deadline to enter the meet is emailed by Thursday, June 1st.  Please contact the Pacific Association office or register online in order to get your athletes registered immediately. THERE WILL BE NO EXCEPTIONS TO THIS RULE.

 

2. Entry fees must be sent to Mike Hammerquist by regular mail and received by the cut-off date of Monday, June 5th.  Entry fee payment must be by money order, or cashier’s check made out to "Pacific Association / USATF."  No personal checks, second/third party checks will be accepted. 

 

3.  Three events maximum for Subbantams, Bantams and Midgets; four events maximum for Youth, Intermediates and Young Men/Women.  A relay is an event.  Only registered PA/USATF clubs can enter a relay team.  Unattached athletes cannot run relays.

 

4. If the club wants a receipt of payment from Mike Hammerquist, a self addressed, stamped envelope must accompany the payment. Meet management will not accept responsibility for lost/late/misdirected mail.

 

5. It is the responsibility of the athlete/team to check in within 5 minutes after the final call for their event. Athletes/teams not checked in by that time will not be allowed to compete.

 

6. All athletes must have bib numbers and hip numbers to compete. Any lost/forgotten bib numbers will be replaced for a $5.00 processing fee.

 

7. All USATF Youth rules will be enforced, along with the Youth By-laws that would apply.

 

8. Only those athletes competing in their event will be allowed on the field. Coaches and Parents/spectators will not be allowed on the field. Any coach or parent in the infield will result in the immediate disqualification of their athlete/team and an automatic grievance will be filed for suspension of 30 days.

 

9. Spikes will be checked at all venues. Only 3/16 inch pyramid spikes will be allowed.  They will be on sale at the registration table if needed.

 

10. Security personnel and qualified medical staff will be on site.

 

11. The team area must be cleaned up by each team before leaving the site.  Plastic garbage bags will be provided at various locations for this purpose.

 

12. A Protest Table will be on site. A $50.00 filing fee must accompany any protest, with the fee being returned if the protest is upheld. If the protest is denied, the fee will become the property of the Youth Committee of the Pacific Association.
