PACIFIC ASSOCIATION

USATF JUNIOR OLYMPIC CROSS COUNTRY CHAMPIONSHIPS

Sunday, November 12, 2006

Golden Gate Park in San Francisco, California

The meet will be hosted by some of the clubs that host other meets this season. 

Bob Shor will be the meet director.  If you have any questions please contact him.  AShorSR@aol.com        or       (707) 539-6787

 

 

Entry Fee: $6.00 per Athlete – Cash or Check (payable to ‘SRX’)

Registration on Day of Meet only – 7:30 AM to 9:00 AM only

 

You must use the ‘2006 USATF Junior Olympic Cross Country Program Individual Entry & Advancement Form’.  This form can be found at the bottom of this information packet.  Near the top of the form, on the line ‘USATF Club Number’ please write ‘38’ and indicate your club number (if you are not a club member please write ‘unattached’).  On the line below it, ‘USATF Association’, please write ‘Pacific’ and to the right, on the same line ‘USATF Region #’, please write ‘14’.  Please fill in the upper portion of the form at the right, the ‘Age Division’ box.  Please check only one box in Section 1.  Do not write anything in Section 2 or Section 3.  Parents and athletes, please sign the lines near the bottom.  Turn this form in, with a copy of your birth certificate stapled to it, and $6 before 9:00 AM.

 

The Course Walk will be at 9:00 AM – The First Race will be at 10:00 AM

 

Races will go from the youngest age group to the oldest age group; girls, then boys.  Shortly after the last runner in a race crosses the finish line the next race will start.  When we get to the Intermediate and Young age groups, if there are fewer than twenty-six (26) runner and fewer than four (4) teams, some or all of these older age group races will be combined.  Scoring will be by each age group.

 

COURSE DISCRIPTION

There are some gentle hills on mostly dirt trails.  There could be some muddy areas, depending on the rain.  No spikes.

 

USATF MEMBERSHIP

Each runner must be a current member of USATF.  If you do not have a current USATF card you may purchase a membership card on the Pacific Association website (www.pausatf.org).  Click ‘Member’; click ‘Join Now’; click ‘New/Renewal Individual Online Registration’ and complete the form.  The cost is $20 and you will need a Visa credit card.  You may become a member at this meet where the cost will also be $20, cash only.

 

AGE GROUPS/RACE DISTANCES

Please be aware that age groups are determined only by the year in which the athlete was born.  The age of the athlete or the school grade in which the athlete is enrolled has no bearing.  Athletes must compete in their own age group.

 

Bantam Girls/Bantam Boys (Born 1996 or later)                       3000 meters

Midget Girls/Midget Boys (Born 1994 or 1995)                        3000 meters

Youth Girls/Youth Boys (Born 1992 or 1993)                            4000 meters

Intermediate Girls/Intermediate Boys (Born 1990 or 1991)        5000 meters

Young Women/Young Men (Born 1988 or 1989)                      5000 meters

 

TEAMS

It takes a minimum of five runners with a maximum of eight runners to enter a team.  All members of the team must be of the same sex in the same age group and all must be registered members of the same registered club.  There is a ‘2006 USATF Junior Olympic Cross Country Program Team Entry/Advancement and Declaration Roster’ that must be filled out and turned in before the race starts.  I suggest that you list every member of your club in that age group on that form.  On the ‘Association’ line, check off the runners who will compete in this meet.  If you add or change runners for the next meet those names must be on the form that you turn in at this meet.  This form can be found near the bottom of this information packet.

 

IN ORDER FOR A TEAM TO COMPETE AT THE REGION 14/JUNIOR OLYMPICS ON NOVEMBER 26TH THE TEAM MUST COMPETE AND QUALIFY AT THE PACIFIC ASSOCIATION/JUNIOR OLYMPICS ON NOVEMBER 12TH.

 

ADVANCEMENT

The first twenty-five (25) finishers and first three (3) teams in each age group will qualify to run in the Region 14/Junior Olympics.  Runners on a qualifying team who finish in the first twenty-five (25) do not change the top twenty (25) finishers.  The Region 14/Junior Olympics will take place in Fresno on Sunday, November 26, 2006.  The information for that meet will appear shortly on this PA website.

 

 

 

THE ONLY INDIVIDUALS WHO MAY COMPETE IN REGION 14/JUNIOR OLYMPICS WITHOUT RUNNING AT THE PACIFIC ASSOCIATION/JUNIOR OLYMPICS ARE AS FOLLOWS:

1) If you compete in a high school sub-section meet or a high school section meet after November 12th you will not be required to run in the PA/JOs on November 12th.  You will be allowed to run in the Region 14/JOs on November 26th BUT YOUR ENTRY FORM MUST BE TURNED IN EITHER AT THE PA/JOS WITH $7 (CASH ONLY) OR BY MAIL (WITH MONEY ORDER OR CERTIFIED CHECK PAYABLE TO ‘REGION 14/JOs’) SO THAT IT ARRIVES AT THE FOLLOWING ADDRESS NO LATER THAN FRIDAY, NOVEMBER 10, 2006.  MAIL TO: PA/JO, 1730 ST. FRANCIS ROAD, SANTA ROSA, CA 95409.  Mail will not be delivered on Nov. 11th

 

2) If you compete in a high school state championship meet after November 12th and before November 26th you will not be required to run in the PA/JOs on November 12th.  You will be allowed to run in the Region 14/JOs on November 26th.  BUT YOUR ENTRY FORM MUST BE TURNED IN EITHER AT THE PA/JOS WITH $7 (CASH ONLY) OR BY MAIL (WITH MONEY ORDER OR CERTIFIED CHECK PAYABLE TO ‘REGION 14/JOs’) SO THAT IT ARRIVES AT THE FOLLOWING ADDRESS NO LATER THAN FRIDAY, NOVEMBER 10, 2006.  MAIL TO: PA/JO, 1730 ST. FRANCIS ROAD, SANTA ROSA, CA 95409.  Mail will not be delivered on Nov. 11th.

 

 

AWARDS

Junior Olympic Medals will be awarded to the more than the first 10 runners in each age group.  There will be a plaque presented to the winning team in each age group.

 

AFTER YOU QUALIFY TO RUN IN THE REGION 14 CHAMPIONSHIPS ON NOVEMBER 26th YOU MUST REGISTER ON NOVEMBER 12th, BEFORE YOU LEAVE GOLDEN GATE PARK

The entry fee to the Region 14 Championships is $7 per runner.  That money, cash only, must be turned in before you leave Golden Gate Park on November 12th.  You will not have to fill out any more forms, as the entry sheet that you turned in for the PA/JO will be forwarded for you.  For your own peace of mind make sure that the information on the form, including the PA/JO results, is correct.

 

REGION 14/JUNIOR OLYMPIC INFORMATION

Information for the Region 14/Junior Olympics will shortly appear on our Pacific Association website.

 

PARKING

Parking is allowed at Golden Gate Park but do not park on the grass, as it does not make the park rangers very happy.  Don’t give them a reason to write out tickets.

 

HOTELS/MOTELS

There are many motels and hotels in the San Francisco area.

 

 

DIRECTIONS TO THE MEET

The meet will be at the south side of the Polo Field, which is at the southwest area of Golden Gate Park in San Francisco.

 

From I-80 – After crossing the Oakland-Bay Bridge take the Ninth Street exit toward Civic Center/Golden Gate Bridge.  Turn left on Harrison Street, turn right on Ninth Street, turn left on Hayes Street, turn left on Gough Street, turn right on Fell Street.  Stay in the two left lanes, staying on Fell Street.  Fell Street will then become Lincoln Blvd.  Follow Lincoln Blvd. and turn right on 25th Street into the park.  Turn left at the stop sign onto Martin Luther King Drive.  At the next stop sign turn right onto Middle Drive.  At the next stop sign you will be at the entrance to the Polo Field.  Do not park on the grass.

 

From I-280 Driving North – Follow the signs to Golden Gate Bridge.  Before you get to the first red light exit at Brotherhood Way.  Take a left under the freeway, turn right on Lake Merced Blvd.  The winding road will then become Sunset Blvd., which will run into Golden Gate Park.  Turn right on Martin Luther King Drive.  At the next stop sign turn left onto Middle Drive.  At the next stop sign you will be at the entrance to the Polo Field.  Do not park on the grass.

 

From the Golden Gate Bridge – Keep to the right at the toll plaza.  Follow the sign to 19th Avenue.  When you go under the tunnel you will be on Park Presidio.  Go almost to the end of Golden Gate Park and turn right on Martin Luther King Drive.  At the stop sign turn right onto Middle Drive.  At the next stop sign you will be at the entrance to the Polo Field.  Do not park on the grass.
