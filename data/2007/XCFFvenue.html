New Venue for Fleet Feet Open Cross Country on November 3

The Third Annual Fleet Feet Open cross country race on Saturday, November 3 has moved to a new venue: Granite Regional Park in Sacramento. The women's 5 km (3.1-miles) and men's 8 km (4.97-mile) will be run on the Park's permanent cross country courses. The park, a converted quarry, features rolling hills, scenic wetlands, and packed dirt and grass trails with plenty of room for passing. The multi-loop course is spectator friendly with excellent viewing from a ridge above the start/finish area.

Granite Regional is a multi-use park with artificial and natural turf soccer fields, a skateboard park, and dog park. The course features plenty of free parking and ideal warm-up and team gathering areas. The course is located just west of Power Inn Road and one mile south of Highway 50.

The Fleet Feet open is race number seven on the Pacific Association of USA Track & Field Grand Prix circuit and the final race before the championship, two weeks later in San Francisco's Golden Gate Park. The women's 5 km begins at 9:30 a.m. The 8 km for men ages 40 and over is at 10:15 a.m. The 8 km for men 39-and-under is at 11:10 a.m. A free pancake breakfast for all entrants is served from 10 a.m. until noon.

The race features a $1,500 prize money purse for open and masters athletes and teams.

Entry is available online at www.ffsac.com. The race page also contains a downloadable entry form. Race fee is $15 if online or postmarked by October 27. Entry fee is $20 after October 27 and race morning.

The event is produced by the Brooks Fleet Feet Racing Team of Sacramento. For more information, contact Doug Thurston, Race Director, Email: raceinfo@ffsac.com, phone (916) 441-1751, website, www.ffsac.com.
